Fontainebleau. Miami Beach.

The venue, confirmed

The most iconic hotel in Miami Beach.

Twenty oceanfront acres on Collins Avenue. Designed by Morris Lapidus in 1954, named to the National Register of Historic Places, and a fixture of American glamour ever since — from Goldfinger to Sinatra at La Ronde to a billion-dollar renovation that preserved every curve.

Fontainebleau serves as the home base for the week, with the summit, dinners, and after-parties all on-property. Offsite programming stays close: the bitcoin++ builder day in Wynwood and the closing Biscayne Bay boat tour are both a short ride away.

Nº 06 — FAQ

Asked and answered.

01Is this for me?

Likely, yes, if you've spent serious time around bitcoin in some capacity: builder, allocator, founder, business owner, writer, operator, or serious learner.

It's also for thoughtful newcomers who want to go deeper. If you care about bitcoin, want better conversations, and are curious about why so many people dedicate their lives, capital, and careers to this network, you'll fit in.

02Is this a crypto conference?

No.

Miami Bitcoin Summit is a bitcoin-only conference. That doesn't mean you have to already agree with every bitcoin maximalist take to attend. In fact, part of the point is to help thoughtful people understand why bitcoin is different from the rest of crypto.

But this is not a place for token pitches, altcoin shilling, or "crypto" panels dressed up as bitcoin education. Many attendees will be bitcoin maximalists, meaning they believe bitcoin is meaningfully different from every other digital asset and that most of the crypto industry is either unnecessary, misleading, or outright harmful.

All are welcome. Shilling is not.

03Why bitcoin-only?

Because bitcoin is different from the rest of crypto in ways that are economic, technical, and ethical.

The fixed supply, proof-of-work, fair launch, lack of a controlling foundation, credible neutrality, decentralization, and monetary purpose all matter. These are not small differences. They are the core of why bitcoin has become the world's most important monetary network.

We'll be diving deeper into those specifics at the conference. The goal is not just to say "bitcoin is different," but to explain why.

One topic. Deeper conversations. No shitcoin tourism.

04How is this different from other Miami bitcoin events — and most bitcoin events more broadly?

The large conferences are great at being large. Miami Bitcoin Summit is intentionally different.

This is a curated, one-day, single-stage event with roughly 300 seats, a premium speaker lineup, and a format designed around actually being in the room together. No expo hall. No competing tracks. No wandering through a massive venue hoping to catch the right person at the right time.

The smaller scale means you are more likely to meet the speakers, have real conversations with other attendees, and leave with relationships instead of just notes.

Think premium, curated, tighter-knit, and high-signal.

05This is the inaugural year. Why should I trust it?

Fair question.

While Miami Bitcoin Summit is new, the organizers have experience putting on bitcoin events, including Bitcoin is for Everyone, a grassroots bitcoin education conference held over the last two years.

For this first year, we're focused on earning trust the right way: a confirmed landmark venue at Fontainebleau Miami Beach, a premium speaker lineup, transparent pricing, a clear refund policy, and a format that is intentionally realistic for year one.

We're not trying to be the biggest bitcoin event in the world. We're trying to create one of the best rooms.

06What does the day actually look like?

Registration, breakfast, and morning coffee begin at 7:30 AM.

The main program runs from 9 AM to 5 PM with keynotes, panels, and fireside conversations on a single stage. There will be proper breaks between sessions, and lunch will be a buffet, with more details to be announced.

Most importantly, lunch will be long enough that you can actually finish a conversation.

The afternoon closes with a keynote talk and closing remarks before the evening Dinner & After Party at Hakkasan. For those who want to keep the night going, late-night LobbyCon continues afterward at Bleau Bar.

07What's included with my ticket?

Every summit ticket includes the full Thursday program, breakfast, lunch, coffee, drinks during the day, and access to all main-stage sessions.

The ticket tiers differ by release timing and price, not by what you receive during the summit.

08Are the side events included?

No. The Welcome Dinner, Dinner & After Party, bitcoin++ builder day, and Biscayne Bay boat tour are ticketed separately.

You can attend as many or as few as you want. The summit is designed to stand on its own, but the side events are there for people who want the full week experience.

See the Side Events section for details.

09What are the side events?

The week is built around a few smaller, more social gatherings:

  • Welcome Dinner for early arrivals, speakers, and sponsors
  • Dinner & After Party at Hakkasan after the summit
  • bitcoin++ Builder Day at The LAB Miami in Wynwood
  • Biscayne Bay Boat Tour departing from Bayside Marina

Each event has its own ticket, capacity, and format. See the Side Events section for details.

10Will I get to meet the speakers?

That's the hope — and it's one of the main reasons we're keeping the event small.

Of course, we can't control every speaker's schedule or guarantee every conversation. But a 300-person, single-stage conference creates a very different environment than a massive event with thousands of attendees, VIP rooms, expo halls, and multiple competing tracks.

The smaller scale gives attendees a much better chance to meet speakers naturally during breaks, lunch, side events, and the evening program.

11Are talks recorded?

Yes. Talks will be recorded, uploaded to YouTube, and made public after the event.

We will also record Q&A, but we will not film the faces of attendees asking questions. The goal is to preserve the value of the conversation while still respecting attendee privacy.

12What's the photo and social media policy?

The stage is fair game. Photograph and post freely.

The audience is not. Please ask before photographing, filming, or tagging fellow attendees, and respect anyone who declines.

We take privacy seriously because many of our attendees do.

13What's the dress code?

Smart casual, Miami edition.

No one needs to show up in a suit, but the summit is at Fontainebleau, the speaker lineup is strong, and you may end up meeting people you actually want to impress. So maybe leave the beach shorts and flip-flops for the pool.

Dress like you're going to a beautiful hotel to meet serious bitcoiners — because you are.

14Where should I stay?

Fontainebleau Miami Beach hosts the summit, and we've secured a preferred-rate room block through January 28. The booking link is in the Venue section.

If you'd rather stay elsewhere, our travel partner Victoire Voyages can help secure rates at many Miami Beach and downtown properties.

15How do I get there?

The summit takes place at Fontainebleau Miami Beach.

Miami International Airport is roughly 25 minutes away by car, and Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport is roughly 45 minutes away, depending on traffic.

The bitcoin++ builder day is at The LAB Miami in Wynwood, about 20–25 minutes from Fontainebleau. The Biscayne Bay boat tour departs from Bayside Marina, about 25–30 minutes from Fontainebleau.

16Can I pay with bitcoin?

Of course.

Checkout supports on-chain bitcoin, Lightning, and card. Lightning is the fastest and our preferred method, but you'll get the same ticket either way.

17Are there special discounted rates, such as group rates or team tickets?

Yes.

We may have discounted options for groups, teams, sponsors, and select partners. For more information, email info@miamibitcoinsummit.com.

18What's the refund policy?

Summit tickets are fully refundable through November 28, 2026.

Tickets are always transferable, so if you can't attend, you can send someone else in your place.

Side event tickets are refundable up to ten days before each event.

For refund or transfer questions, email info@miamibitcoinsummit.com.

19Are there sponsorship opportunities?

Yes, but selectively.

This is not an expo-driven event. We're only interested in sponsors that fit the room and add value to attendees.

See the Sponsors section or email info@miamibitcoinsummit.com for more information.

20Accessibility, dietary, and other accommodations?

Fontainebleau is ADA-accessible, which is one reason we chose it.

We'll do our best to accommodate dietary needs, accessibility needs, nursing rooms, prayer space, quiet rooms, or anything else that helps attendees have a better experience.

Please email info@miamibitcoinsummit.com with any specific requests. The earlier we know, the easier it is to help.

21Still have questions?

Email info@miamibitcoinsummit.com or DM @miamibitcoinsummit on X.

A real person answers, usually within a day.
